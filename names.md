Since "Solar Hub" is already taken on the Play Store, you need an Arabic name that is catchy, memorable, and reflects the full scope of the platform. Here are several categorized suggestions for your app:

1. Modern & Catchy (Brandable & Short)
These names are short, easy to remember, and sound like a modern tech startup.

شَمْسِيَّات (Shamsiyyat) – "Solar Things / Solarities". Simple, catchy, and covers everything related to solar energy.
طاقتي (Taqaty) – "My Energy". Very personal and user-friendly for B2C users.
نِبْراس (Nibras) – "The Lamp / The Light Source". A very elegant, classic Arabic word that implies providing light/solutions.
سُطُوع (Sutou') – "Brightness / Shining". A positive, energetic word.
وات (Watt) – Just "Watt" written in Arabic. Short, universal, and directly tied to electricity.
2. Hub / Platform Replacements (Descriptive)
These translate the idea of a "Hub" or "Center" seamlessly into Arabic.

مِنَصَّة شَمْس (Manassat Shams) – "Sun Platform". Sounds very professional and business-oriented.
عالم الطاقة (Alam Al-Taqa) – "World of Energy". Good for a lifestyle or community-heavy app.
مِحْوَر الطاقة (Mihwar Al-Taqa) – " Energy Axis / Energy Hub". The most direct and formal translation of "Energy Hub".
دليل الطاقة (Daleel Al-Taqa) – "The Energy Guide". Great if the app focuses heavily on education, calculators, and community advice.
سوق الشمس (Souq Al-Shams) – "Sun Market". Perfect if the primary focus of the app is the Store, POS, and Suppliers features.
3. Business & Professional Focused
Since your app has strong B2B features (Accounting, Invoices, POS, Company Dashboard).

أعمال الطاقة (A'mal Al-Taqa) – "Energy Business".
روّاد الطاقة (Ruwwad Al-Taqa) – "Energy Pioneers". Sounds like an exclusive platform for professionals and top tier tech.
شريك الشمس (Shareek Al-Shams) – "Sun Partner". Excellent if your main pitch is to help suppliers and companies manage their businesses and find clients.
4. Direct Translations (English loanwords localized)
If you still want a modern/English-adjacent feel.

سولار تك (Solar Tech)
طاقة هَب (Taqa Hub) – Mixing Arabic (Energy) and English (Hub), very common in Gulf tech startups.
برو سولار (Pro Solar) – To indicate it's a professional tool.
